Lab 4 - Pseudocoding and Problem-Solving

Pseudocoding a simple task: making chicken katsu

  1. Prep chicken breasts or tenders
  2. Crack an egg into a bowl
  3. Mix the yolk into the white
  4. Pour some panko bread crumbs into a different bowl
  5. Coat the chicken in the egg
  6. Coat the chicken in the bread crumbs
  7. Heat vegetable oil in a pan
  8. Fry the chicken for about 3 mins on medium-low heat, then flip
  9. Serve with a sauce or sriracha and rice

Pseudocoding a game: word hunt

  1. Display a 5x5 grid of squares
  2. Randomly generate a letter for each square
  3. When a player drags between 2 or more letters with their finger, a line strikes through them
  4. Calculate every possible combination of words that can be made with the letters
  5. Words must be at least 3 letters long and can be found in the dictionary
  6. Players can connect every 2 letters any way vertically, horizontally, and diagonally
  7. The line between letters is red
  8. The line turns green when the player swipes over a word
  9. The player gets that word when they lift their finger
  10. Three-letter words are 100 points, four-letter words are 400 points, and each letter on adds 400 points
  11. A 1 minute timer begins counting down as soon as the game starts
  12. When the timer runs out, the scoreboard for both players are shown
